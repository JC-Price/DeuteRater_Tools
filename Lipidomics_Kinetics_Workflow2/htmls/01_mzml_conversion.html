Welcome to Kinetic Lipidomics

Welcome to Kinetic Lipidomics — a framework designed to enable kinetic mass spectrometry analysis of lipid turnover across the lipidome. This program mirrors the workflow described in Nielsen et al., 2026, allowing users to reproduce our analyses with our published raw data {add link for our raw data}, or carry out new kinetic studies on their own datasets.

The core concept of this workflow is:

  • Monitoring the replacement of protium (1H) by deuterium (2H) in lipid molecules over time to quantify molecular replacement in vivo.

This approach assumes:

  • That you, your lab, or your collaborators have performed an isotope labeling time-course across multiple biological subjects, or have access to data of that form. In Nielsen et al., 2026, each experiment included six time points (0, 0.25, 1, 4, 16, and 32 days), with three biological replicates at the earliest and latest time points, and two replicates at each intermediate point. We recommend adopting a similar design for followers of this workflow.

The first step is the preparation of raw mass spectrometry data for downstream kinetic analysis.

All raw instrument files must first be converted into the standardized .mzML format for compatibility with Isobar Handler and DeuteRater. We recommend using ProteoWizard’s MSConvert tool for this conversion, available at:

https://proteowizard.sourceforge.io/

After installing ProteoWizard, locate the folder containing msconvert.exe (for example: C:\Program Files\ProteoWizard\ProteoWizard 3.0.23137.ff55254). We recommend enabling vendor peak picking during conversion, as this produces centroided spectra and accelerates later analysis steps.

Although MSConvert can be operated through its GUI or command line, we standardize its usage via an automated helper tool to ensure reproducibility. Click the "Generate MSConvert Batch Command" button below to launch a small GUI that allows you to:

  • Select the path to msconvert.exe
  • Specify your operating system
  • Toggle vendor peak picking

Click Generate to produce a ready-to-run command. Copy this command to your clipboard and paste it into your system’s terminal. Navigate to the directory containing your raw mass spec files using cd (for example: cd "C:\Users\MyPC\Documents\MS_files") and run the command. A new folder will automatically be created containing your converted .mzML files.

For advanced users, additional conversion options and parameters are described here:

https://proteowizard.sourceforge.io/tools/msconvert.html
